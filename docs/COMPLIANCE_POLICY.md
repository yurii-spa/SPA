# SPA Family Fund — Compliance Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** 2026-06-20  
**Jurisdiction:** Private family fund — not regulated as a public investment vehicle  
**Maintained by:** Fund Manager (Yurii Kulieshov)

---

## Overview

SPA Family Fund is a private family investment fund. Its purpose is to pool
capital from direct family members of the fund manager and deploy it into
DeFi yield strategies via the SPA system. It is not open to the general public.

All investors must satisfy the eligibility, KYC, and AML requirements set out
in this policy before capital is accepted.

**Important:** The fund is currently in the paper-trading period. No real capital
has been deployed. This compliance policy applies from go-live date (target
2026-08-01) onward and is published in advance for governance purposes.

---

## Investor Eligibility

To invest in SPA Family Fund, a person must:

1. Be a direct family member of the fund manager (spouse, parent, sibling,
   child, or first cousin).
2. Complete KYC verification (see below).
3. Sign the investment agreement (`docs/legal/`).
4. Be at least 18 years of age.
5. Not be a Politically Exposed Person (PEP) or subject to international sanctions.

The fund manager may, at their sole discretion, decline any application.

---

## KYC Requirements

### Required Documents

All investors must provide:

- **Government-issued photo ID** — valid passport or driver's licence.
- **Proof of address** — utility bill, bank statement, or official letter dated
  within the last 3 months, showing full name and residential address.
- **Self-declaration of source of funds** — written statement explaining the
  origin of investment capital (employment income, savings, inheritance, sale of
  assets, etc.).

### Submission Process

1. Submit documents via secure channel (encrypted email or in-person delivery).
   Documents must not be transmitted via unencrypted SMS or messaging apps.
2. Fund manager conducts manual review, verifying:
   - Document authenticity (not visibly altered or expired).
   - Name matches across all documents.
   - Address matches proof of address document.
3. Approval or rejection communicated within **5 business days**.
4. Rejected applicants receive a written reason (where permitted by law).

### Annual Renewal

KYC documents must be renewed annually. The fund manager will contact investors
60 days before expiry. Failure to renew results in suspension of profit
distributions until updated documents are received.

## AML Policy

### Source of Funds

- All investors must declare the source of funds in writing.
- Funds from illegal, criminal, or unexplained origins are refused.
- The fund manager may request additional evidence if the stated source of funds
  is unclear or inconsistent with the investor's background.

### Accepted Transaction Methods

- **Bank transfer only** — wire transfer or SEPA bank transfer from a verified
  personal or corporate bank account held in the investor's name.
- **No cash transactions** under any circumstances.
- **No third-party transfers** — funds must originate from an account in the
  investor's own name. Third-party transfers (e.g. payment by a friend or
  business) are refused without exception.

### Suspicious Activity

- If at any point the fund manager has reason to believe that funds are connected
  to money laundering, fraud, or other criminal activity, the investor's
  participation will be suspended immediately.
- Suspicious activity will be reported to the relevant financial intelligence
  authority in accordance with applicable local laws.
- Investors are not permitted to "tip off" other parties about a suspicious
  activity report.

## Data Protection

### Storage and Access

- KYC documents and investor personal data are stored securely (encrypted at
  rest) on devices controlled by the fund manager.
- Access is restricted to the fund manager and, where legally required, to
  regulatory authorities.

### Third-Party Sharing

- Investor personal data is not shared with third parties for commercial
  purposes.
- Data may be disclosed to legal or regulatory authorities if required by law.

### GDPR (EU Investors)

For investors residing in the European Union:

- Personal data is processed on the lawful basis of contractual necessity
  (performance of the investment agreement).
- Investors have the right to access, correct, and request deletion of their
  personal data, subject to legal retention requirements.
- Data retention period: the investment agreement term plus 5 years, or as
  required by applicable law.

## Dispute Resolution

Disputes between the fund manager and investors are resolved through direct
negotiation between family members. If direct negotiation fails, the parties
may agree in writing to involve a mutually acceptable third-party mediator.
Formal legal arbitration is the last resort and governed by the jurisdiction of
the fund manager's primary residence.

---

## Policy Amendments

This policy may be amended by the fund manager with **30 days' written notice**
to all investors. Material changes (e.g. changes to AML thresholds or data
retention periods) require investor acknowledgement before taking effect.

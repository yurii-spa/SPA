## 1. Strategy Naming — Final Recommendation

### Decision: **Variant A — Preserve / Core / Max Yield**

**Rationale:**

Each word describes what the strategy *prioritizes*, not what it *promises*. This is the core compliance distinction.

| Option | Why it works | Why not the others |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Preserve** | Signals priority without implying guarantee. "Preservation-focused" is an allowed formulation. Instantly legible to traditional allocators. | "Conservative" (Variant C) is overused, generic, and carries implicit safety connotations. |
| **Core** | Classic institutional term from the core/satellite allocation framework. Implies a diversified, systematic approach — not retail, not speculative. | "Balanced" (Variant B) is fatally overloaded in traditional finance (it means 60/40 equity/bond). Would confuse the exact audience we're targeting. |
| **Max Yield** | Honest and transparent about the objective. "Max" sets expectations upward without claiming certainty. Pairs naturally with the required "variable APY, not guaranteed" disclosure. | "Growth" (Variant C) implies equity upside, which is conceptually wrong for stablecoin yield and would mislead DeFi-unfamiliar allocators. |

**Risk gradient legibility test:**  
*Preserve < Core < Max Yield* — any non-crypto reader understands this hierarchy in under 3 seconds.

**Approved strategy names:**
- `Preserve` — target ~6% APY (future profile, not yet active)
- `Core` — ~10% APY (paper tracked since June 10, 2026 ✓)
- `Max Yield` — target ~15% APY (future profile, not yet active)

## 4. Messaging Framework

### Main Value Proposition (one sentence)

> SPA is a systematic, risk-gated DeFi yield manager built for family offices that want verifiable operations — not a black box.

**Alt version (for technical audiences):**
> A deterministic allocation engine that runs documented risk gates before every DeFi trade, with a paper-tracked performance record published daily.

---

### Proof Points

These three claims are factually defensible and should anchor all sales, marketing, and onboarding materials.

**Proof Point 1 — Verified Track Record**  
*"Paper-tracked since June 10, 2026. Equity curve logged daily, no retroactive edits."*  
Why it builds trust: It's auditable. We're not asking allocators to take our word for it — the record exists and can be examined.

**Proof Point 2 — Deterministic Risk Policy**  
*"Every allocation must pass a documented risk policy before executing. No LLMs, no discretion, no overrides."*  
Why it builds trust: It removes "key person risk" from the risk management layer. The rules are public. What they are is less important than that they exist and are enforced consistently.

**Proof Point 3 — Full Deployment Transparency**  
*"At any moment, you can see exactly where capital is deployed, what each protocol pays, and why positions are sized the way they are."*  
Why it builds trust: This is what institutional allocators expect and almost no DeFi product delivers. Transparency is the moat.

---

### Forbidden Claims

The following phrases create legal exposure or make factual misrepresentations. They must not appear in any customer-facing material — website, emails, pitch decks, or onboarding documents.

| Word / Phrase | Why it's forbidden |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **safe** | Implies absence of risk; creates regulatory liability (SEC, CFTC, MiCA) |
| **guaranteed** | Direct legal liability — APY in DeFi is structurally variable |
| **protected** | Implies insurance or downside guarantee that does not exist |
| **risk-free** | Categorically false for DeFi; even stablecoin yields carry smart contract risk, depeg risk, and liquidity risk |
| **capital protection** | Implies principal guarantee; this does not exist in any SPA strategy |
| **earn X%** (without qualifier) | Unqualified yield claims imply certainty; always accompany with "target," "paper tracked," or "variable" |

---

### Approved Claims

These formulations are accurate, compliant, and effective. Prefer these over alternatives.

| Approved Phrase | Usage context |
|----------------|---------------|
| `capital-preservation focused` | Preserve strategy description, value prop |

### Verified vs. Target vs. Future — Distinction Guide

This is the most important distinction to maintain in all copy. Blurring these lines creates compliance risk.

| Category | What it means | How to label it |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Verified paper performance** | Core strategy, equity curve logged daily since June 10, 2026 | "Paper tracked since June 10, 2026" |
| **Target APY** | Aspiration based on current protocol yields; not a promise | "Target ~X% APY" or "Target profile" |
| **Future strategy profiles** | Preserve and Max Yield — designed but not yet active | "Target profile — not yet active" |

**Rule:** Never display a yield number without one of these three qualifiers.

## 5. How SPA Works — 3 Steps

> Designed for a non-technical allocator. No DeFi jargon. Each step answers the implicit question: *"But what does that actually mean for me?"*

---

### Step 1 — Choose a risk profile that matches your objectives

SPA offers three strategies: Preserve, Core, and Max Yield. Each targets a different balance of yield and risk exposure. Select the one that fits your allocation goals and time horizon. Minimum allocation: $25,000 USDC.

*What this means for you: You're not locked into a single pool or protocol. You're choosing a risk framework — and the strategy handles the rest.*

---

### Step 2 — A risk policy reviews every allocation before it executes

Before any capital moves, SPA's deterministic risk engine checks the proposed allocation against a set of documented rules: protocol TVL minimums, per-protocol concentration limits, and portfolio-level drawdown thresholds. If a trade doesn't pass, it doesn't execute — no exceptions, no manual overrides.

*What this means for you: Your capital doesn't move into a protocol that hasn't passed a documented filter. The rules are public. You can read them.*

---

### Step 3 — Track your position with full transparency

Every trade, every yield accrual, and every daily equity snapshot is logged and visible in your dashboard. You can see exactly where capital is deployed, what each protocol pays, and how the portfolio has evolved since inception.

*What this means for you: No black box. No "trust us." A running record you can audit at any time.*

## 6. /trust Page — Tone Guidance

### Purpose of the page

The /trust page exists to preemptively answer the questions a serious allocator will ask before they commit capital. The goal is not to impress — it's to remove doubt.

**Core principle:** State facts plainly. Let the facts do the work.

---

### Tone Principles

**Do:**
- Lead with specifics, not adjectives. *"All state files are written atomically — tmp file + replace, no partial writes"* is more credible than *"our infrastructure is reliable."*
- Use "you can verify" language wherever possible. Show, don't tell.
- Be specific about who has access to what, what is logged, and what cannot be changed retroactively.
- Acknowledge what SPA *doesn't* do yet (go-live is August 2026) — this honesty is a trust signal, not a weakness.

**Don't:**
- Use defensive language: *"we would never," "rest assured," "you can trust us."* These phrases signal that someone anticipated mistrust and tried to pre-empt it.
- Make unqualified claims about infrastructure maturity.
- Over-explain technical details on the main page — link to them for allocators who want depth.

---

### Writing About Custody

**Avoid:** *"Your funds are safe with us."*  
**Use instead:** *"SPA does not hold custody of your assets. Capital remains in your wallet or in the smart contracts of the underlying protocols at all times."*

Why: Factual, specific, and answers the question the allocator is actually asking.

---

### Writing About Admin Roles

**Avoid:** *"Our admin controls are secure."*  
**Use instead:** *"Administrative access to cycle parameters requires [X]. All changes are logged and timestamped in [location]. No admin action can retroactively modify performance records."*

Why: Specificity is the credibility. Vague reassurance is its own red flag.

---

### Writing About the Risk Policy

**Avoid:** *"We take risk seriously."*  
**Use instead:** *"SPA's risk policy is a deterministic rule set — not an opinion, not an algorithm that can be tuned by the system at runtime. Every parameter is documented in [RiskPolicy v1.0]. A change to any parameter requires a new Architecture Decision Record and a versioned snapshot."*

Why: This tells the allocator exactly what "risk management" means here, which is what they actually want to know.

---

### /trust Page Structure Suggestion

1. **How capital moves** — step-by-step with no abstraction
2. **What the risk policy does** — and doesn't do
3. **What is logged and where** — specific file names / endpoints
4. **What SPA cannot do** — explicit non-capabilities (can't move funds without passing risk gate, can't override a blocked trade)
5. **Current status** — paper trading, go-live target, what changes at go-live
6. **Questions?** — direct contact, not a chatbot
